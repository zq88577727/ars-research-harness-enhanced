# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "Cardiometabolic and lifestyle correlates of HbA1c-defined undiagnosed diabetes among self-reported non-diabetic U.S. adults: a cross-sectional analysis of NHANES 2017-2018" for consideration as an Original Research article.

This study uses nationally representative NHANES 2017-2018 data to estimate the weighted prevalence of HbA1c-defined undiagnosed diabetes among U.S. adults who self-reported no prior diabetes diagnosis and to characterize demographic, anthropometric, cardiometabolic, and lifestyle correlates. The analysis focuses on a screening-gap population rather than adults with diagnosed diabetes and uses survey-weighted methods appropriate for NHANES.

The manuscript's main contribution is a conservative, interpretable profile of undiagnosed diabetes among self-reported non-diabetic adults. We report that HbA1c-defined undiagnosed diabetes affected 2.17% of the weighted analytic population, while HbA1c-defined prediabetes affected nearly one-quarter. Waist circumference, non-HDL cholesterol, obesity, and selected race/ethnicity categories were associated with higher prevalence or odds, with careful interpretation of race/ethnicity as social categories rather than biological causes.

All analyses used publicly available, de-identified NHANES data. No additional participant contact occurred. The manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere.
